Attack Melee

Damage : Weapon Skill:
Melee (Brawn, Agility)

Failure: inflict no damage, recieve full damage

Standard Success: You hit your target for full damage, but your opponent hits you for half damage.

Great Success: You inflict full damage on your target, and your opponent does not get an action. 

Extraordinary Success: Your attack is a complete success +

  • add 1d6 damage,
  • knock down your opponent,
  • stun your opponent, or
  • inflict 1 Wound.

Range Attack

Damage : Weapon
Skill: Missile/Throw(Brawn, Agility)

Failure: You do not harm your target, and :

  • an opponent with a ranged attack strikes you for full damage,
  • an opponent with a ranged attack fires at one of your allies (and that character or NPC must make a defence test),
  • an opponent within melee range strikes you for maximum damage, or
  • you lose 1d3 additional ammo. 

Standard Success: You strike your target for full damage, but:

  • an opponent with a ranged attack strikes you for half damage, 
  • an opponent with a ranged attack fires at one of your allies (and that character or NPC must make a defence test),
  • an opponent within melee range strikes you for full damage, or
  • you lose one additional ammo. 

Great Success: You inflict full damage on your target.

Extraordinary Success: Your attack is a complete success. +:

  • add 1d6 damage,  • knock down your opponent, 
  • stun your opponent, 
  • inflict 1 Wound,
  • you have an opportunity for a special effect, such as pinning your opponent.

Psychic Attack

Damage : 1D6
Skill: Psychic

Any character can initiate a psychic attack against an Enemy with the Incorporeal benefit. Additionally, characters with the Psychic Assault Special Ability can initiate a psychic attack against anyone. Psychic attacks always ignore armour.

Failure: You do not harm your target, and:  

  • an opponent inflicts psychic damage on you, or
  • an opponent with a ranged or melee attack strikes you for full damage, or 
  • an opponent with a ranged attack fires at one of your allies (and that character or NPC must make a defence test).  Either way, you suffer disorientation from the psychic effort, and all your psychic attacks have a −2 penalty until you take a short rest; disorientation penalties are cumulative. 

Standard Success: You strike your target for full damage, but:

  • an opponent inflicts half psychic damage on you,
  • an opponent with a ranged or melee attack strikes you for half damage, 
  • an opponent with a ranged attack fires at one of your allies (and that character or NPC must make a defence test). Either way, you suffer disorientation from the psychic effort, and all your psychic attacks have a −1 penalty until you take a short rest; disorientation penalties are cumulative. 

Great Success: Your attack is a complete success. 

Extraordinary Success: Your attack is a complete success. +: 

  • add 1d6 damage,
  • stun your opponent,
  • prevent your opponent from using psychic attacks for 1d6 rounds, or
  • inflict 1 Wound.

Cast Spell

Skill: Spellcasting test.

Failure: The spell does not take effect, but the caster must spend spell points(s) or lose memory of the spell. 

Standard Success: The spell takes effect, but there’s a complication determined by the Game Master:

  • You must spend one extra spell point; otherwise, you’re “strained” by spellcasting, suffering a −2 penalty to all Spellcasting tests until you have a short rest. Penalties from strain effects are cumulative.
  • You’re placed in danger and suffer the full effects of an attack from an Enemy (either an Enemy with a missile weapon or a spellcaster). 
  • Your spell is only partially successful (e.g., target takes half damage, or the spell has half duration). 

Great Success: Your spell takes full effect with no side effects. 

Extraordinary Success: The spell takes full effect with no side effects +:

  • If you have become strained from spellcasting, you can remove the penalty for one strained effect.
  • The spell point cost is decreased by 1.
  • The spell effect increases in some way as determined by the GM (such as increased damage or effect area).

Assist

Assist by using a skill.

Roll 1d6. if the result is <= than your skill you can give the die result to the assisted character.

(A melee warrior cannot assist an archer who is attacking an Enemy that’s out of range)

If multiple people assisting only the highest assist roll is used as a bonus.

Defend

You can only take defence tests with  +4 against melee/range attacks

Protect

Full action and usually requires a Melee (Agility) test, although other (creative) alternatives are possible.
If you’re attempting to protect another character that is involved in melee, you must declare your protect action before that character is attacked; its effects last until your next action.

Failure: You fail to protect the other character.

Standard Success: You can halve the damage from one attack on the character you are protecting, or you can protect them from all damage and take the full amount of damage yourself. 

Great Success: You block all the damage from one incoming attack on the person you are protecting. 

Extraordinary Success: You block all damage from one incoming attack on the person you are protecting. The GM may allow an additional effect, such as  causing the attacker half damage.

Disengage

Athletics (Agility) test against a difficulty equal to the Enemy’s Threat; 

Failure: You do not disengage, and you take full damage from your opponent. 

Standard Success: You can disengage and move up to half your Speed, but you cannot take another full action that turn. Your opponent strikes you for half damage. 

Great Success or better: You disengage without harm; you can then take an action and/or move.

Recovery

You can attempt a recover action to restore lost Life.
When making a recover action, you can take no other action, but you can make a defence test when you are attacked.

  • You can take a number of recover actions each day equal to your Resolve. This resets when you take a full rest.
  • If you have taken any Wounds, you cannot take a recover action.
  • If a recover action is taken during a combat, you must make a Fortitude (Mettle) test against Difficulty 14.
    • Failure: You do not recover any Life.
    • Standard Success: Recover 1d3 Life.
    • Great Success: Recover 1d6 Life.
    • Extraordinary Success: Recover 1d6+3 Life.

A recover action taken during a short rest requires no roll and is automatically a Great Success.

Armor Repairing

You’ll need at least one hour + proper equipment (which might include a forge for heavy armour) + one armour repair kit.
Tinker (Agility) test. The Difficulty = 8 + (the number of armour points that need to be repaired).

  • Failure: No armour points are restored, and the armour kit is used.
  • Standard Success: Restore 1d6 armour points; the armour kit is used.
  • Great Success: You restore 2d6 armour points and the armour kit is used, or you restore 1d6 armour points and the armour kit is not used.
  • Extraordinary Success: Restore 2d6 armour points; the armour kit is not used.

Shields

+2 bonus to defence or protect test.

After an attack or defence test is made but before the GM rolls damage, you can declare that your shield is taking the hit. Roll 1d6. If you roll a 5 or 6, the shield is still intact; if you roll a 1–4, the shield is destroyed. Either way, the shield absorbs the damage.

If the damage inflicted from a single source is higher than the listed armor amount on the shield, the shield only blocks that amount and then automatically breaks.

Helmets

If a melee or missile attack would reduce you to zero (0) Life, you can take the blow on the helm and take no damage. After the GM rolls damage, if the attack would reduce you to zero (0) Life, you can declare that you are taking the hit on your helm. Roll 1d6. 6 = helm remains intact. 1–5 = helm is destroyed. 

−2 penalty to all Notice tests while wearing the helm.

Movement

Free action to move, or full action to sprint (movement x 3)

Light Fate

The maximum= character’s level + 2.
If you roll a 6 on the Kismet Die while at maximum, you can spend that earned Fate immediately.

You begin each session with 1 Light Fate.

Spend Fate to affect a roll, activate special ability if the roll is successful or s part of a spell or when activating the Special Quality of a magic item.
You can spend Light Fate once for any given opportunity

  • Add a bonus die
  • After you roll for a test, you can spend to reroll one of the dice, but you must keep the new result. (You cannot do this if you spent Fate to add to the roll.)
  • Spend to increase a Standard Success to a Great Success. You cannot do this if you have already spent Fate to modify the die roll
  • Spend to activate one of your Special Abilities.
  • Spend to activate an item’s Special Quality.
  • Some spells require expenditure.
  • When you fail a death test spending Fate means that you pass the test for that round.

Dark Fate

The Game Master begins the session with 1 Dark Fate token for each player. Maximum Dark Fate = num. of players *  2 + the level of the highestlevel character.

  • Spend to increase an Enemy’s Threat rating by 1d6 for one round. This must be done at the beginning of the round and can’t be done as a reaction to a character attacking that Enemy.
  • The Game Master can spend to activate an Enemy’s Special Ability. Unless a Special Ability specifically states otherwise, the use of a Special Ability replaces an Enemy’s normal attack.
  • The Game Master can spend to activate effects in special areas
  • The Game Master can spend to activate an item’s curse effects (listed under Special Qualities).

Wounds

Max wounds = Mettle or Resolve (whichever is higher).

You remain unconscious until one of the wounds is healed, even if your Life is fully restored. Wounds can only be healed through natural healing, first aid, or magical effects.

Death and Dying

When you reach zero (0) Life, your character is unconscious and on the verge of death. Each round after this happens, you must make a death test to see if your character is still alive. This is a Difficulty 8 test using Mettle or Resolve (whichever is higher); each additional round after the first, the Difficulty increases by 1.

  • General Succes and 6 on the Kismet Die you stabilize and awaken on your own after 1 minute with 1 Life.
  • If you fail this test, your character is dead.

If neither condition is met, you’re still dying.

Any kind of healing spell or magical effect immediately stabilises a dying character, or a Heal (Insight) test against Difficulty 14.

Short Rest (15 min)

First Aid

Restore life and might heal Wounds they’ve suffered within the last hour. (You cannot use first aid on yourself). Only one attempt per person after combat.

Healing (Insight) testagainst Difficulty 14.

  • Failure: No Life is restored.
  • Success: The target heals 1d6 Life.
  • Great Success: The target heals 2d6 Life, or the target heals 1d6 Life and 1 Wound.
  • Extraordinary Success: The target heals 2d6 Life and 1 Wound.

Refitting Armour

Remove slashes (/)

Recovering Life

One recover action. Same as  combat action (and counts against the total you can make in a day) but it is automatically a Great Success, restoring 1d6 Life.

Recovering Spell Points

Roll 1d6; you recover that number of spell points. You can do this once per short rest.

Long Rest

Eight hours of uninterrupted rest, six hours of sleep. One long rest per 24-hour period. After a long rest, all lost Life and all spell points are recovered. However, Wounds are not recovered; (first aid, natural healing, or magical means).

Natural Healing

Once per day, attempt to heal 1 Wound after taking a long rest.
Fortitude (Mettle) test against Difficulty 14; you must get a Great Success or better. The Difficulty for this test is reduced by 2 for each day you don’t succeed.

Tending to Wounds

Heal (Insight) test against Difficulty 14;

  • Standard Success or better on the test, the character you’re tending gets a +2 bonus to their natural healing test.
  • Great Success or better, the character you’re tending can increase a Standard Success on a natural healing test to a Great Success. You can tend to multiple characters during a long rest, although the difficulty is increased by 2 for each additional character tended.
